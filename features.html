Features

These are lists of some core features of Lumeer. The lists are not extensive so if you think we miss your favorite feature, do not hesitate to ask for it!

Click on the small question marks to learn more.

Views

Views allow you to see the same data through various lenses depending on your current task. There is a wide palette of options and we keep adding more.

  • Search results
    You can search for any of your data like you would do in a general search engine. The data are then displayed in several tabs very similar to your favorite search engine - All, Document, Links, Views - depending on your query. Moreover, you always get hints from Lumeer while typing your query.
  • Smart data table
    Almost like a spreadsheet table but allows you to specify column constraints, conenct data from multiple collections, display sub-tables of related records, add headers and footers with some functions, add function columns, specify conditional formating for rows and columns and many more.
  • Details
    Displays all the details about a particular record including access rights, change history, linked records, data constraints etc.
  • Links explorer
    Allows you to traverse through linked documents like you were browsing a file system. Moreover allows you to easily find related records and create new links either individually or in large batches.
  • Contingency table
    Very frequently, it is necessary to put data into some context. Simply see how two different things relate to each other. This is easily done with a contingency aka pivot table. You can define aggregation functions and even edit the source data directly through the pivot table.
  • Calendar
    Do your data specify time dimension? Real world objects do not have just some placement but also an exsitence in time. If this is your case, you can naturally see your data mapped in a calendar with the abily to edit the time information by simply dragging the items. Map multiple different types of records in a single calendar view to put into a context.
  • Map
    GPS and other geo-location services are very popular and many data are collected together with their position. Take an advance of this and display your records in a world map or your own floor plan. Put multiple different types of records in a single map view to get contextual information. Drag records by a mouse to directly modify their location values.
  • Gantt chart
    Use records about utilization and available resources and display them in a Gantt chart. Drag records in the Gantt chart to directly edit them.
  • Wiki
    Sometimes, data need some explanation. Or maybe you want enrich your story by real data. For both of these cases, you can create a Wiki-like document and mix paragraphs of text with data records. This form is ideal for creating formal documents like Product Requirements.
  • Agile board
    Do you want to map your records into columns based on some attribute values? This is what Agile board view is good for. Add one more attribute and create swim lanes. Add a time dimension and watch your board evolve in time. Drag and drop items to directly edit your records.
  • Chart
    An image is worth a thousand words. Show your data in a plot, line, bar or pie chart. Use multiple dimensions. Configure visual parameters.
  • Mind map and graph
    Work with your records like with pieces of paper on a table. Draw links between them as needed, zoom in and out, move them along, switch between various types of links. This is the most free-form data manipulation you have ever seen.
  • Data experimentor
    Sometimes, you just need to run an experiment with your data. Answer some what if-s, figure out what happens when you change the input parameters, create a series of computations one leading to another. Avoid loosing values in some random spreadsheet cells and run your experiment with a natural flow and order.

Collaboration

Collaboration with your colleagues and friends is critically important and Lumeer facilitates this with the following features.

  • Projects and users management
    Within your organization, you can create sandboxes, projects, invite users, and give them access to the projects. Projects can separate logical areas or can share data between them. Switch easily and quickly between projects as needed. Work with multiple projects in multiple web browser tabs.
  • Data versioning and history tracking
    Create snapshots and versions of your data in a given point in time. Track who and when made changes.
  • Fine grained access control
    Specify as many details about who can read, write and configure any resource in the system as you need. You can fine tune your configuration based on individual users, or you can simply set your data readable to a certain group, overyone in your project or even make it public.
  • Comments and discussions on record level
    Create discussions on a collection or individual record level. Instead of sending emails with links a descriptions, simply start your conversation directly in the system. In the end, you can ask Lumeer to send you a copy of the conversation to your email.
  • Sharing of data and views
    Collaboration helps us deliver greater results. Share your data and views, ask your colleagues for their feedback and suggestions for changes.

Business Processes

Computers are very good at automation and processing large data sets. Lumeer enables this by providing easy to understand business processes. You can start off by creating your own checklist of tasks, add some automated data manipulation steps later, and then share with all of your colleagues when you are ready.

  • Process triggers
    Lumeer can help you automate some of your tasks. Just let him know when to start a task. Should it be based on new data record created or updated? Should it be upon receiving an email? Or do you want to start the task periodically?
  • Process versioning
    When updating an automated process, you can specify what happens to already running instances of the old version of the process. Either it can complete according to the old definition, or it can be restarted in the new format or completely cancelled.
  • Data events
    Lumeer can observe data modifications and fire processes or send notifications based on these.
  • E-mail events
    Lumeer can understand some email so you can ask him to do a favour for you. He can even send you a response when he is done.
  • Business rules
    Specify rules that can judge some record values for you and let these values to be automatically added to your data.
  • Data manipulation tasks
    In a process, Lumeer can automatically retrieve data, store data (create or update a record), and execute functions on the data.
  • Human tasks
    Even in an automated process there are times where a human interaction is needed. Lumeer can tell you when this happens and provides a task to you with all the related contextual data. Or you can just create a checklist for yourself and your colleagues for more complex tasks. And who knows, maybe in the future you process wil get a wide adoption through sharing!

Data

Lumeer supports an extensive number of data manipulation features that are expected from applications working with data.

  • Documents, collections, links
    Basic resources in Lumeer are collections (like sheets or tables in spreadsheets) that contain documents (like rows or records). Individual documents contain attributes (columns) and these have different values for each document. Although there is no strict format for documents in a single collection, they mostly have the same attributes. Documents can be linked together and these link can have arbitrary attributes and values. For example, your doughnuts recipe can link to all-purpose flour. You cannot cook crispy and soft doughnuts based on this information. This is why your link has an attribute called amount with the value of 500g. And btw. Lumeer understand units so he can compute that this is 3.5 cups or 17.14oz.
  • Smart hints for better data management
    Lumeer is here to help you with your data operations. So he observes what you do and gives you advices and suggestions on how to best structure your data. He can also ask you some questions for you to help him understand your data.
  • Data constraints
    By default, you can enter any data in any document attribute you want. But maybe you just want to make sure that a date is entered, or that a value from an enumeration is used. No matter what, you can tell Lumeer to keep an eye on this and make sure that the specific attribute contains only valid values.
  • Batch operations
    You might need to join or split a column in your data. You might want to recompute some values. Or you might need to create a new collection based on your existing data. Lumeer is happy to help you with any batch operation you want.
  • Data imports
    Onboarding is always painful. Lumeer understands this and tries to make it as smooth for you as possible. You can import data from various formats you might be already using like spreadsheets, text files (CSV), and relational databases.
  • External collections
    Lumeer likes open data. He can automatically mirror your favourite source of information to a collection in your project.
  • Easy copy'n'paste
    You most probably rely on copy'n'paste in most of your applications. Why would Lumeer be any different? Simply select any piece of your data and paste it somewhere else.
  • Special types - tags, categories, geolocation
    We know what are the typical data people use and what are the formats most applications cannot handle efficiently. Lumeer knows these types and makes your daily duties easy.
  • Data migration
    You can move data between collections, projects or even organizations. That's it.
  • Sandboxes
    Not sure how to solve a particular problem and are you afraid of breaking a project? Start a sandbox, copy some sample data or a few collections there and give it a try. In the end, you can copy'n'paste your solution to the original project. The sandbox gets deleted automatically after some time.
  • Collection archives
    Not sure about deleting some old data permanently? Archive them in a collection that is omitted from search results and other operations and try to live without it. Over time you'll see whether it is safe to really delete these data.
  • Search results snapshots
    Take a snapshot of search results as they are at the moment. This is like taking a picture, screenshot or printing a page. Later you can compare the updated data to their previous state.
  • Query expressions
    Use results of a search query to search for some other data or as a configuration parameter in your view.
  • REST API
    Develop your own automated tooling to work with data stored in Lumeer over its public REST API.
